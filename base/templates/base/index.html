William Ledbetter An Engineer, etc. I Build and Learn Things

William Ledbetter is in his second year of PhD study after receiving a Bachelor of Science in Aerospace Engineering from The University of Alabama, summa cum laude. He has been interested in space for as long as he can remember, and learned how to code by reading the TI-83 Plus manual. He played tuba at the South Carolina Governor's School for the Arts and Humanities, and sang in St. Peter's Basilica for the first mass under Pope Francis. As an undergraduate, he participated in a senior design project in conjunction with NASA and Dynetics, performing mission architecture analysis for proposed missions to Mars. William is currently researching autonomous mission design for swarm exploration of asteroids, including landing and rendezvous.
